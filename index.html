She published seven autobiographies, three books of essays, and several books of poetry, and is credited with a list of plays, movies, and television shows spanning more than 50 years. She received dozens of awards and more than 30 honorary degrees. Angelou is best known for her series of seven autobiographies, which focus on her childhood and early adult experiences, the first of which, I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings (1969), brought her international recognition and acclaim.

A singer, dancer, and performer in her early years, Angelou worked internationally before becoming a writer. She was a close friend and associate of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and Malcolm X, and her work often reflected themes of identity, family, racism, and resilience. Later in life, she was celebrated as a powerful voice of African American culture and history, with her writings emphasizing strength, dignity, and hope. In recognition of her contributions, she was awarded the National Book Award, three Grammy Awards, and the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2011. Maya Angelou remains an enduring symbol of courage, creativity, and the transformative power of words.
